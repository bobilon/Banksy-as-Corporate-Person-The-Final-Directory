# Banksy-as-Corporate-Person-The-Final-Directory
The consolidated final map of the Banksy conglomerate, drawn from the UK Companies House register. Revises and expands my original two-part directory with the full Hirst cluster, the HENI/Hage successor architecture, and the sales-side wind-down vehicles. Every entity tagged DOCUMENTED, STRUCTURAL, or HYPOTHESIS by connection strength.
This is the consolidated, final version of my original two-part "Banksy as Corporate Person" posts. It keeps the original argument and adds every company I've since put in play, each marked by how strongly the public record ties it to the Banksy enterprise. A note on that marking, because it's the whole method:

[DOCUMENTED] — the connection rests on filed records: shared directors, shared registered office, PSC declarations, incorporation and dissolution dates, share registers. Anyone can verify it at Companies House.
[STRUCTURAL] — the entity's filed attributes (address, SIC code, timing) make a Banksy role likely, but the role itself is read off the structure, not stated in a filing.
[HYPOTHESIS] — my reading. Where I name people or assign a company a secret function, that's my investigative conclusion, not something the register confirms. I flag it so you always know which is which.

The identity of the Makers — who actually made the work — is nowhere in the public record, exactly as a tradecraft-smart operation would arrange. My candidates appear here as my high-confidence hypotheses, not as facts the filings establish.

Part 1 — The Conglomerate
Banksy is a mystery designed to remain unsolved — a goal the arts-and-entertainment press has helped along by repeating the disinformation baked into the project from the outset. That included at least three false-flag Banksy candidates (Gunningham, Del Naja, Hewlett), a counterintelligence-style tactic that kept the casual mystery-fan engaged while discouraging serious work on "Who is Banksy?" The same writers have done a poor job on the corporate side too — which, since the UK's Companies House register went electronic in 2015, takes no great feat of research for anyone who knows company searches and art-production businesses.
When I went looking myself, early on, I turned up a dozen-plus companies that play roles in the Banksy project. Processed against Banksy's history, they go a long way toward telling the story of Banksy as a business — and toward tracing the human story without which there'd be no business story at all.
What still astounds me is that I'm the one doing it, nearly ten years after the changeover. Even Wikipedia misstates the broad strokes, flatly calling Pictures on Walls (POW) a print-manufacturing-and-sales business that closed in 2017. The record says otherwise: POW's direct-to-consumer print business was the front for the Banksy Artwork limited partnership — the parent of everything material to the artist's fine-art production. (It does not include the publishing and film divisions; those are separate but obviously interrelated parts of the conglomerate.) [DOCUMENTED] POW's own annual filings make the front-business reading hard to argue with: after the 2017 closure of the print business, year-to-year cash carry climbs off a roughly £800K baseline toward the £2.5M range, and the accounts show reserves rising to £3.47M by 2025 — a company doing more, not less, after its "only business" supposedly shut.
Private-company reporting is minimal, so exactly how much flowed through the Banksy families of companies can't be determined. But the public record shows POW was the parent of four Banksy subsidiaries and that it handled bookkeeping and payroll for several corporate loan-out companies — the loan-outs of key people on the project. Larger subcontractors with their own accounting departments were handled at arm's length under long NDAs; the smaller, more central businesses were brought in-house during key transactional periods.
The Durban mechanism — and this is the spine of the whole map. [DOCUMENTED] Banksy's accountant, Simon Durban, sat on the boards of those loan-out companies for exactly the spans when their business merged with POW's or when they were transacting an exit. The reason is a clean piece of corporate hygiene: by sitting on both boards, Durban held a fiduciary duty to each party, which forecloses any conflict-of-interest claim between the parent and the contractor that could become a lawsuit. Track when Durban joins and leaves a board, match it to Banksy's history, and the deals start to show their outline. (On loan-out companies generally: see the Wikipedia entry on loan-out corporations.)
Durban is also credited as Film Production Accountant on Exit Through the Gift Shop [DOCUMENTED] — the rare skill set for running the finances of a complex art-production business, where the working knowledge already includes contract law and corporate structure. His charge ran well past a normal film: the books of four obvious Banksy subsidiaries (plus one not-disclosed) beyond POW — Pest Control Office, Paranoid Pictures Film Company, Dismaland, Gross Domestic Product, Tout Suite Pictures — and the books of several Banksy-affiliated loan-outs: Lazinc, GBFTCU, Farmer, Outline Design and Services.
His full appointment history (2005–2019) is one Companies House officer search away.

The Roster — in order of incorporation
PRO-ACTIF COMMUNICATIONS LIMITED — inc. 22 October 1998 (formerly Identity Crisis Limited, 22 Oct–11 Nov 1998). Other publishing activities. Durban never sat on this board.
[STRUCTURAL/HYPOTHESIS] My read: the Banksy publishing imprint. It handled editing for Banksy's books and official Banksy-related titles. After the 2005 monograph Wall and Piece (Century/Random House), Pro-Actif followed with the three-volume Untitled series (2008–2010); the Banksy tie was plausible but not certain then. It became clear in 2014 with the official-"unofficial" follow-up, You Are an Acceptable Level of Threat (distributed via Gingko Press), since updated twice with later works. The "Identity Crisis" founding name is itself suggestive — Pest Control asks identity tips be titled "Existential Crisis." That the name rhymes is documented; that it's a deliberate signal is my reading.
GBFTCU LIMITED — inc. 12 November 1998, dissolved 16 July 2010. Artistic & literary creation. Durban accountant 1 Apr 2006 – Mar 2010. (Formerly Underwater Records, then Electric Earth Records.)
[HYPOTHESIS] This is, in my reading, the artist-of-record's first loan-out company — incorporated right as Banksy first appeared in print (October 1998, Sleazenation). My strong suspicion is that it began as a contract-player vehicle while the artist-identity brand was owned by POW, until a 2007 corporate shake-up forced the parent to grant the artist a full-partner stake and contractual control of Banksy's future production. This is also where my lead suspect sits: I believe, with high confidence but without a filing to prove it, that Lucy McKenzie is the beneficial owner of GBFTCU. No insider — including McKenzie or her gallerist — has seen fit to deny it. That is my vote, not the register's.
FARMER LIMITED — inc. 2 November 2005. Specialized design activities. Durban accountant 19 Aug 2008 – 11 Mar 2010.
[STRUCTURAL/HYPOTHESIS] Loan-out of Hugo Farmer, in Banksy art production since the early 2000s. With Lazarides' exit, the UK art-production management role opened up; the Durban-on-board dates fit the cradle-to-grave lifespan of the 2009 Banksy v. Bristol Museum show, which is my read of what Farmer ran.
LAZINC LIMITED — inc. 2 April 2004. Unclassified business support. Durban Finance Director 14 Feb 2006 – 19 Mar 2009; accountant 1 Aug 2007 – 14 May 2008. (Formerly Lazarides Limited, then Finlaw 457.)
[DOCUMENTED + HYPOTHESIS] Steve Lazarides became a partner in April 2004, likely bringing Stage-1 funding via his Lazinc partner, Qatari billionaire Wissam Al Mana, for projects like Exit and ongoing POW operations. [HYPOTHESIS] Lazarides was removed by early 2008 after losing a power struggle with the Artist; his stake was repaid (or he took a golden parachute) via a fall-2007 auction grossing the company ~£3M. He kept gallerist rights to artists developed alongside Banksy. The board dates are documented; the power-struggle reading is mine.
PICTURESONWALLS LIMITED (05143539) — inc. 2 June 2004, active. Unclassified business support. Durban Finance Director 23 May 2005 – 7 Nov 2019.
[DOCUMENTED] The parent of the Banksy Art & Event partnership — a ~20-year arrangement that has recently unwound, letting partners keep whatever works they held outright. [DOCUMENTED] PSC since the 2016 regime: a single 75%+ controller at a time — Mark Samuel Chambers (Glendale, California) to 2 August 2019, then Mark Howard Stephens (London). Reserves £1.16M (2015) → £3.47M (2025); staffing 0–2; only fixed asset a £1 investment. [HYPOTHESIS] The founding deal likely dates to 1998, with angel funding and production support from Damien Hirst by late 1999 for the brand's start-up phase.
PEST CONTROL OFFICE LIMITED (06472402) — inc. 14 January 2008. Arts-facilities operations. Durban accountant 14 Jan 2008 – 7 Nov 2019.
[DOCUMENTED] After the 2007 shake-up severed Lazarides from sales, PCO became the artist's management/authentication body — the front function being authentication of Banksy works. It is staffed (8–9 people), carries a £23,750 "Art" asset, and its net assets ran ahead of POW every year across the decade, £2.74M (2015) → £5.70M (2024). [DOCUMENTED] In January 2020, POW was registered as a PSC of PCO — the shop took control of the regulator. [HYPOTHESIS] That PCO's cash carry has now surpassed POW's is, to me, further sign that Banksy as an ongoing commercial concern has ended — rights/residuals outlasting sales.
PARANOID PICTURES FILM COMPANY LIMITED (06641316) — inc. 9 July 2008. Other service activities n.e.c. Durban accountant 9 Jul 2008 – 7 Nov 2019.
[STRUCTURAL/HYPOTHESIS] Not the parent of Banksy filmed entertainment (which I haven't found). Incorporated around the close of Mr. Brainwash's LA show; the timing and PCO-borne costs fit it shouldering Exit's post-production — the most substantial part of Banksy's directorial claim, since the reality-scripted film needed no director except for the Banksy-interview scene. My read: a brand-and-slate promotional investment that didn't return profit to the parent until the early 2020s.
POSITIVE ACCOUNTING SOLUTIONS LIMITED (07802117) — inc. 7 October 2011, active. Bookkeeping. Durban accountant and owner, 2011–present.
[DOCUMENTED] Durban's own loan-out and holding company. Here is the corrected, filed figure (my original estimated £560K from memory; the accounts are exact): PAS carried £595,000 of acquired goodwill, amortized straight-line at £59,500/year over ten years to zero, reaching nil by 2024 — while its retained reserves climbed inversely to ~£1.04M, and a flat £1,000,000 "amounts owed by group undertakings" appears as the goodwill runs off. [HYPOTHESIS] My reading: that goodwill represents the stake Durban earned after fulfilling his launch-years contract — value the booming Banksy market would only price later. The measured liquidation to zero this cycle tells me the partnership has reached its programmed end. Paired with 2023's Cut and Run debuting no new work — a curtain call — it strongly suggests the commercial run is over, by contract or by the artist retiring the brand. (Note: the filing says "acquired goodwill," i.e. PAS bought an income stream; what that stream was, the accounts don't name. The schedule is documented; the meaning is my inference.)
OUTLINE DESIGN AND SERVICES LIMITED — inc. 3 February 2012. Other service activities n.e.c. Durban accountant 3 Feb 2012 – 6 Jan 2017.
[STRUCTURAL/HYPOTHESIS] Once the launch-years design team dispersed, this company, in my read, served the artist's design needs for the three big 2010s pop-ups: Better Out Than In (2013), Dismaland (2015), the Walled Off Hotel (2017). The board dates roughly bracket that window.
GROSS DOMESTIC PRODUCT (GDP) LIMITED — inc. 27 June 2012. Other business support n.e.c. Durban accountant 27 Jun 2012 – 7 Nov 2019.
[DOCUMENTED + HYPOTHESIS] Its early function is unclear to me. [DOCUMENTED] In 2019 the entity was used to build a quasi-commercial basis for defending Banksy's copyright without forcing disclosure of the artist's identity.
TOUT SUITE PICTURES LIMITED — Motion-picture production. Durban accountant 13 May 2013 – 13 May 2015.
[HYPOTHESIS] Possibly the production entity for the 2014 HBO documentary on the 2013 NYC residency. The board dates fit; the function is my guess.
DISMALAND LIMITED — Unclassified business support. Durban accountant 1 May 2015 – 7 Nov 2019.
[DOCUMENTED + HYPOTHESIS] Obviously the 2015 show's production company. [DOCUMENTED] Reporting-law changes left its majority ownership split between Durban and the artist's manager (ex-Lazarides assistant) Holly Cushing — my read is that this happened after the show's art assets had been transferred to the parent. Little money on its books since the year after the event.

Part 2 — Pictures on Walls as Banksy's Corporate Cloak
POW is the logical starting point for any rigorous look at the Banksy companies, because nearly everything connects to or ran through it — including the loan-outs whose books its accounting department kept for finite intervals. It's as close to a corporate mothership as the Banksy mystery has, alongside the standalone publishing imprint (Pro-Actif) and the still-undiscovered film mothership whose existence can be inferred from the POW subsidiary Paranoid Pictures.
The fragmentation strategy — and why it worked. [DOCUMENTED in effect, HYPOTHESIS in intent] By splitting into many small companies, the conglomerate did two things. First, it buried its operations through the paper-record era — a 15-letter corporate name does real shielding work in a non-searchable archive — until the 2015 electronic changeover. Second, it kept any single company under the size thresholds that would have forced full disclosure: profit-and-loss, cash flow, the lot. That shielded the true scale and profitability of the enterprise. The effect is visible in the filings; the deliberate intent is my reading, though it's a reading the pattern makes hard to avoid.
The Hewlett front. [DOCUMENTED + HYPOTHESIS] POW ran a well-advertised front business — print manufacturing and direct sales — that claimed to be its sole enterprise and appeared to be owned by Jamie Hewlett (Tank Girl, Gorillaz) from 2004 to 2017. [HYPOTHESIS] My read: Hewlett was a high-profile nominee — a frontman who "volunteered" as shareholder of record for the beneficial owners, serving cross-promotion for himself and misdirection for the project, expanding the Banksy fanbase toward the Comic-Con and EDM crowds. Both Banksy and Gorillaz date to 1998. The idea of Hewlett secretly helming Banksy while keeping a high-profile career is absurd; his apparent majority ownership simply sold the fiction that POW was only what it claimed.
The 2016 gaffe and the cleanup. [DOCUMENTED] A 2016 rule change on who may act as a nominee shareholder caught POW off guard. Tellingly, the 2016 annual report carries "esq" after Durban's signature — the only time in his fourteen-year tenure — which I read as an acknowledgment the filing was out of compliance and they knew it. [HYPOTHESIS] The cleanup: the 67-share POW stake (the only outstanding shares since 2008) was transferred to Glendale attorney Mark Samuel Chambers, backdated to 2012 to explain Hewlett's departure four years ahead of the bad 2016 filing; in 2019 the proxy moved to London attorney Mark Howard Stephens, and the print front-business closed that year. [DOCUMENTED] Chambers-then-Stephens at 75%+ is on the PSC register; the Hewlett-held-it-first-and-it-was-backdated reading rests on the pre-2016 share record, which survives only in scanned returns — so I tag it as my reconstruction. [DOCUMENTED] POW's reserves more than doubled across the closure — the fact that finally made POW-as-parent impossible to ignore.

The Business Model and the Makers — my hypothesis, stated as such
This corporate map is the prelude to my Breaking Banksy investigation, which opened over two years ago with a three-division model — Art, Publishing, Film — each with a chief. [HYPOTHESIS — these are my candidates, not the register's findings, and I hold them at high confidence but as hypotheses:] Lucy McKenzie as artist of record; Jefferson Hack handling publishing and publicity; Sacha Baron Cohen as Exit's showrunner and the on-camera/spoken Banksy persona; and, added since, Damien Hirst as lead equity partner — the role Charles Saatchi played for the YBAs. After 170-plus Instagram posts and 50-plus Reddit threads, my initial hypothesis still stands, and I think the corporate record proves it correct in its most important part — but "proves the structure" and "proves the names" are different claims, and only the first is on the register.
The natural next deep-dive is Lazinc — a Rosetta Stone for reading the other loan-outs, because matching each loan-out's known owner to the company explains why each entered and left Durban's ledger, from both the parent's and the owner's side. And a study of POW's outstanding share ratios before and after Lazinc's time as an equity partner appears, to me, to confirm the partner-and-department structure my research suggested — including a scenario where the artist takes control of POW's partnership as largest shareholder in 2007, at odds with the former partners for the rest of the run.
That last reading — the share-ratio reconstruction of a partner structure — is the one I hold most loosely, because the early share register isn't cleanly extractable. It fits. It isn't filed.
